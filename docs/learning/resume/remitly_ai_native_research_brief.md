# Research Brief — Remitly AI Native Software Engineer

## Source role
**Role:** AI Native Software Engineer  
**Company:** Remitly  
**Location:** Seattle, WA  
**Compensation listed:** $124,000–$155,000 base salary  
**Work mode:** In-office  
**Primary posting referenced:** Built In Seattle listing for Remitly AI Native Software Engineer  
**Supporting company sources referenced:** Remitly Careers / Life at Remitly / Investor Relations

---

## Why this research matters

Before rewriting a resume for this role, the job must be treated as a signal source rather than generic recruiting prose. This posting is unusually explicit about what it wants, and the distinction matters:

This is **not** a conventional software engineering role centered on manual code output volume.

It is much closer to an **AI-first product builder** role: someone who can translate ambiguous needs into buildable specifications, direct AI coding agents, ship end-to-end product surfaces, evaluate generated output critically, and iterate using metrics while preserving quality, security, privacy, and reliability.

That framing should govern the resume.

---

## 1. What profile the role is actually looking for

### Core profile
The advertised profile is best described as:

**AI-native full-stack software engineer with product judgment**

Not:
- pure backend specialist
- pure frontend specialist
- classical feature-ticket implementer
- research scientist
- prompt hobbyist with weak engineering depth

### What makes the profile distinct
The role emphasizes that the engineer will:
- direct **AI agents** to build products and solutions
- ship **complete applications**
- build **workflow automations**
- build **basic agents**
- work across **frontend, backend, data, and infrastructure**
- be measured by **speed of delivery** and **robustness of shipped output**, not commit volume

That means the ideal candidate is a builder who can:
- operate across the stack
- reduce ambiguity
- use AI as a real production multiplier
- maintain engineering standards under accelerated delivery
- think in terms of product outcomes, not just implementation tasks

## 2. Highest-signal responsibilities to mirror in the resume

These are the responsibilities worth reflecting most directly in experience bullets.

### A. End-to-end product delivery
The role wants someone who can build and ship entire product surfaces, not just code isolated tickets.

**Resume evidence to surface:**
- full-stack feature ownership
- shipping production-ready user-facing systems
- deployment-ready implementation
- ownership across frontend, backend, data, and integration boundaries

### B. Translating needs into buildable specifications
The engineer is expected to participate in customer conversations, identify real needs, and convert them into something concrete enough to build.

**Resume evidence to surface:**
- turning vague stakeholder requirements into scoped plans
- spec-driven development
- converting operational pain points into workflow/software solutions
- writing technical artifacts that reduced ambiguity before implementation

### C. AI-mediated execution
The role explicitly centers use of **AI coding agents** as a normal working method.

**Resume evidence to surface:**
- using AI-native workflows in implementation
- prompt/system workflow design
- agentic development patterns
- structured spec → implementation pipelines
- validation/governance around generated code or outputs

### D. Instrumentation and iteration
The posting mentions measuring what ships using metrics such as usage, conversion, and error rates.

**Resume evidence to surface:**
- observability
- instrumentation
- analytics-informed iteration
- post-launch refinement using measured outcomes
- reporting/monitoring surfaces tied to business or operational performance

### E. Quality and trust judgment
The engineer is expected to evaluate AI-generated output against standards for security, privacy, reliability, and quality.

**Resume evidence to surface:**
- reviewing generated output critically
- governance, validation, auditability, safety checks
- fintech/compliance-adjacent rigor
- quality gates, standards, and production hardening

## 5. Company research — what Remitly likely values in practice

### Company mission and business context
Remitly’s stated vision is to transform lives with trusted financial services that transcend borders. Its public product posture emphasizes secure, simple, and reliable money movement for immigrants and families sending money internationally.

That matters because this job’s engineering expectations align with a **trust-sensitive fintech environment**:
- security matters
- privacy matters
- reliability matters
- quality matters
- customer empathy matters

This is not an environment where “AI-native” means reckless speed alone. It means using AI to accelerate delivery **without violating trust**.

### Public company scale signal
Remitly’s recent investor materials indicate meaningful operating scale, including:
- billions in annual revenue
- millions of active customers
- substantial remittance volume
- positive profitability metrics

That implies a mature operating environment where product and engineering decisions are expected to survive real-world usage, not just demo conditions.

## 6. Remitly values that should influence resume positioning

Remitly publicly highlights the following cultural values:
- Customer centricity
- Be an owner
- Bias for action
- Continuously improve
- Constructively direct
- Stand for excellence
- Dive deep
- Reimagine what’s possible
- Earn trust through integrity

Not all of them need to be mirrored explicitly, but several should be visible in how the resume reads.

### Most relevant values for this role

#### Customer centricity
The role mentions customer conversations and identifying real needs.

**Resume implication:** Show evidence of solving operational/user pain rather than just implementing features mechanically.

#### Be an owner
This role expects end-to-end initiative.

**Resume implication:** Emphasize ownership, decisions, shipped outcomes, and cross-functional initiative.

#### Bias for action
The posting explicitly values shipping speed.

**Resume implication:** Show acceleration, reduced time-to-delivery, shorter iteration loops, or pilot-focused execution.

#### Stand for excellence
Generated output still must meet quality bars.

**Resume implication:** Highlight hardening, validation, governance, testing, quality gates, and production thinking.

#### Dive deep
The role is not superficial prompting; it requires understanding systems and workflows.

**Resume implication:** Surface examples of diagnosing complexity, structuring ambiguous systems, or building rigorous internal frameworks.

#### Reimagine what’s possible
This is the part of the culture that most cleanly maps to AI-native workflows.

**Resume implication:** Show where you changed the working model itself, not merely where you used a new tool.

#### Earn trust through integrity
Critical in fintech and critical in any role where AI-generated output is shipped.

**Resume implication:** Make reliability, auditability, judgment, and restraint visible.

## 9. Practical resume implications

### What the summary/header should sound like
The resume should likely frame you as something close to:

**AI-native full-stack software engineer who builds operational software end-to-end, using structured AI-assisted workflows, strong product judgment, and quality-focused engineering discipline.**

That is much closer to the target role than a generic “full stack developer” summary.

### What the experience bullets should do
Each strong bullet should ideally communicate some version of:

**problem → ownership → build approach → shipped system → measured effect**

Even if the metric is modest, that structure is stronger than a tool list.

### What the project section should not do
Do not let the resume over-rotate into:
- academic AI language
- buzzword-heavy agent discourse
- architecture theater without delivered outcomes
- generic stacks without why they mattered

This role wants builders, not ceremonial technologists.

## 11. Bottom-line takeaway

The strongest interpretation of this posting is:

Remitly is hiring a **production-minded AI-native product engineer**, not just a conventional SWE with a few AI buzzwords added on top.

A good resume for this role should therefore foreground:
1. **AI-native development workflow**
2. **end-to-end full-stack ownership**
3. **specification and ambiguity reduction**
4. **instrumentation and iteration**
5. **quality, security, privacy, and reliability judgment**
6. **customer/problem orientation**
7. **action bias without recklessness**

If the resume makes those seven ideas visible, it will align far better with the actual role than a generic software engineering resume.

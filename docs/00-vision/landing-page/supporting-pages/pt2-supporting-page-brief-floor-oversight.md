# PT-2 Supporting Page Brief — Floor Oversight

**Date:** 2026-04-10  
**Purpose:** Define the content direction for the first supporting page in the landing-page architecture: **Floor Oversight**.

This artifact exists to keep the page grounded in implemented system reality, not generic SaaS theater.

---

## 1. Role of This Page

The main landing page now acts as a **contained narrative spine**.

Supporting pages exist to expand one operating loop at a time without overloading the homepage.

**Floor Oversight** is the first exemplar page.

Its job is to answer:

> What does it mean to run a live casino floor with current visibility?

This page should not become a general product dump, a player page, a cashier page, or a compliance manual in disguise.

---

## 2. Core Promise

**See the floor as it is now, not after the shift is over.**

This is the page’s central claim.

The page should frame Floor Oversight as a live operational surface for:

- current floor visibility
- exception monitoring
- performance movement during the shift
- drill-down from property view to pit/table inspection

---

## 3. Domain Boundary

Floor Oversight, in system terms, is primarily composed of:

- shift dashboard
- checkpoint & delta tracking
- alerts
- coverage quality
- metrics drill-down

This is the backbone of the page.

If a section does not strengthen one of those five ideas, it likely does not belong here.

---

## 4. What the Page Is Not

This page is **not** primarily about:

- Player 360 depth
- rating slip mechanics
- cashier workflows
- compliance workflow detail
- setup/import/onboarding
- loyalty program operations
- admin settings as a whole

Those can be referenced where contextually needed, but they are not the subject of the page.

---

## 5. Audience

Primary audience:

- pit managers
- floor supervisors
- operations leads

Secondary audience:

- administrators who need property-wide visibility

This is not a cashier-facing page and should not be written as one.

---

## 6. Page Narrative

The page should follow a simple operational story:

1. See the floor at a glance
2. Mark a checkpoint during the shift
3. Monitor movement, coverage, and exceptions
4. Drill into pits/tables when needed
5. Stay ahead of issues before the shift is over

This is the correct narrative mode.

Not “here are our dashboard widgets.”
Not “here are twelve metrics in boxes.”
Not “look how much UI we built.”

---

## 7. Primary Content Pillars

## 7.1 Live Floor Picture

This is the opening proof block.

The system currently supports a casino-wide oversight layer that includes:

- casino-wide shift metrics
- win/loss trends
- alerts
- coverage quality
- floor activity radar
- active players list
- shift time window selection
- drill-down into pits and tables

This is the strongest content cluster for the page and should lead.

### Page purpose of this pillar

To establish that Floor Oversight is not retrospective reporting.

It is a live operating view.

---

## 7.2 Checkpoint & Delta Tracking

This deserves featured treatment.

A checkpoint is a mid-shift snapshot used to measure change over time.

The page should present this as one of the most distinctive capabilities in the system:

- mark the state of the floor
- continue operating
- measure what changed since that point

This is more than a metric.

It is a monitoring rhythm.

### Page purpose of this pillar

To show that the product supports active management during the shift, not passive observation after the fact.

---

## 7.3 Coverage & Exceptions

Oversight only matters if it helps surface what needs attention.

This pillar should explain that the system is built to surface:

- gaps in floor coverage
- notable changes in activity
- alert conditions
- operational exceptions requiring inspection

This section should stay operational, not abstract.

Avoid language like “advanced analytics.”

The point is practical:

> what needs attention right now?

---

## 7.4 Drill-Down: From Property View to Pit View

The page should show the movement from the top-level shift dashboard into more focused inspection.

The supporting surface here is the **Pit Dashboard**, which includes:

- table-level view
- seat map
- player list
- inventory
- table analytics

This section is important because it prevents “oversight” from sounding vague.

It makes clear that oversight is not just summary metrics.  
It leads to actionable inspection.

---

## 7.5 Shift Rhythm

This page should translate the product into an operating loop.

A clean sequence is:

- route into the shift dashboard
- create a checkpoint
- monitor the shift
- investigate movement and alerts
- inspect pits/tables when needed

This gives the page motion and keeps it from reading like a static brochure.

---

**Live floor visibility during the shift**

See KPIs, coverage, alerts, and change over time from one operating surface.

- View the product

## 8.2 Section 1 — The Floor Picture

### Goal
Show the property-wide oversight surface.

### Content focus
- KPIs
- trends
- activity

- time-window framing
- alerts

Present-tense, live, operational.

---

### Goal
Feature checkpoint and delta tracking as a differentiated mechanism.

### Content focus
- snapshot the current state
- measure what changed
- use it during live operations

### Visual recommendation
This section deserves one of the strongest visuals on the page.

---

## 8.4 Section 3 — Drill Into the Pit

### Goal
Show how oversight moves from summary to inspection.

### Content focus
- pit dashboard
- table-level visibility
- seat map
- inventory context
- player presence
- analytics where operationally useful

---

## 8.5 Section 4 — Oversight in Practice

### Goal
Translate the product into a real operating sequence.

### Content focus
Use a simple live-floor narrative:

- arrive on shift
- open the floor view
- set a checkpoint
- monitor variance and gaps
- inspect where needed

This section should feel like work, not marketing.

---

## 8.6 Final CTA

### Goal
Continue the narrative chain.

Recommended CTA direction:
- Explore Session Tracking
- See Cash Accountability
- See Audit Compliance

This page should not dead-end.

It should hand off naturally into the next operating loop.

---

## 9. Tone Guidance

The tone of this page should be:

- operational
- current-tense
- calm
- specific
- restrained

It should avoid:

- generic “business intelligence” language
- feature cramming
- abstract “visibility and insights” filler
- compliance sprawl
- overexplaining the entire system

The page should feel like it was written by someone who understands floor operations, not by a marketing intern trapped in a dashboard vendor.

---

---

## 11. Exclusions for Drafting

Do not let the page get hijacked by these topics:

- full Player 360 explanation
- detailed cash workflow
- MTL/CTR deep detail
- onboarding wizard explanation
- CSV import detail
- loyalty mechanics
- role-permission matrix expansion

Those belong to other supporting pages.

---

## 12. Success Criteria

The Floor Oversight page is successful if:

1. it is understandable in under 15 seconds
2. it feels like an operating loop, not a feature list
3. it clearly centers the shift dashboard and checkpoint model
4. it shows how the user moves from broad visibility to focused inspection
5. it leaves room for adjacent pages to carry their own detail

---

## 13. Final Summary

**Floor Oversight** should establish one clean idea:

> The system gives operators a live picture of the floor, a way to mark change during the shift, and a path to inspect issues before they harden into end-of-day surprises.

That is enough.

Do not let the page swell into a second homepage.

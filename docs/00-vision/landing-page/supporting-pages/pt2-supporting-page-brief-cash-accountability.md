# PT-2 Supporting Page Brief — Cash Accountability

**Date:** 2026-04-10  
**Purpose:** Define the content direction for the supporting page **Cash Accountability**.

This artifact exists to keep the page grounded in implemented system reality and to prevent it from swelling into a hybrid of cashier workflow, compliance manual, and generalized financial reporting page.

---

## 1. Role of This Page

The supporting-page architecture expands one operating loop at a time.

**Cash Accountability** is the page that explains how the system captures and preserves money movement on the floor while it is happening.

Its job is to answer:

> How does the system record, confirm, and preserve cash activity so it can be trusted later?

This page should not read like an accounting suite, and it should not become a compliance page wearing a cashier badge.

---

## 2. Core Promise

**Record cash movement while it is happening.**

This is the page’s central claim.

The page should frame Cash Accountability as the operating loop that connects:

- buy-ins
- cash-outs
- reversals and voids
- fill / credit confirmations
- drop acknowledgements
- chip inventory context
- bank and rundown visibility
- staff attribution on money events

The strength of the page is live accountability.

Not retrospective reporting.  
Not abstract “financial visibility.”  
Not generic control language.

---

## 3. Domain Boundary

Cash Accountability, in system terms, is primarily composed of:

- buy-in recording
- cash-out recording
- large-amount confirmation
- live threshold feedback as supporting context
- voids with reason and note
- fill / credit confirmations
- discrepancy notes
- drop acknowledgements
- chip inventory by denomination
- drop / fill tracking
- bank summary
- rundown reporting
- staff attribution on cash events

If a section does not strengthen one of those ideas, it likely does not belong here.

---

## 4. What the Page Is Not

This page is **not** primarily about:

- full MTL / CTR workflow explanation
- general audit philosophy
- floor-wide KPI monitoring
- Player 360 depth
- setup, onboarding, or player import
- loyalty ledger and reward mechanics
- table opening/closing custody logic as a primary topic
- generalized back-office reporting

Those can be referenced when necessary, but they are not the subject of this page.

---

## 5. Audience

Primary audience:

- pit managers
- floor supervisors
- cashiers
- operators responsible for recording or confirming money activity

Secondary audience:

- administrators evaluating whether the system can preserve money movement with enough structure to review later

This page should feel operational and controlled, not back-office and abstract.

---

## 6. Page Narrative

The page should follow a simple live-floor sequence:

1. Record the cash event
2. Confirm the movement where required
3. Preserve reversals and discrepancies without losing the trail
4. Tie money movement back to table context
5. Review the shift with enough structure to trust what happened

This is the correct narrative mode.

Not “here are our finance features.”  
Not “here is a compliance checklist.”  
Not “look at our cashier console tabs.”

---

## 7. Primary Content Pillars

## 7.1 Capture Cash in Motion

This is the opening block.

The system currently supports:

- buy-in recording
- cash-out recording
- large-amount confirmation
- live threshold feedback during entry

This section should make clear that the page is about recording cash movement at the point of activity, not reconstructing it from memory later.

### Page purpose of this pillar

To establish that cash accountability starts with live entry, not end-of-shift cleanup.

---

## 7.2 Preserve Reversals and Exceptions Cleanly

Money handling is rarely perfect.

The system supports:

- cash-out voids
- required reason capture
- audit note attachment
- discrepancy notes where needed

This section should frame reversals and exceptions as part of the controlled record, not as embarrassing edge cases hidden off to the side.

### Page purpose of this pillar

To show that the system can absorb mistakes and corrections without smearing the history of what happened.

---

## 7.3 Confirm Movement Across the Floor

This is where the page becomes more than patron-facing transaction entry.

The system supports:

- fill confirmations
- credit confirmations
- discrepancy notes
- drop acknowledgements

This section should explain that cash accountability includes movement between operational roles and stations, not just one operator recording a patron buy-in.

### Page purpose of this pillar

To show that the product preserves handoff and confirmation steps across the operating surface.

---

## 7.4 Tie Cash Activity Back to Table Context

Cash events should not float loose from the table they belong to.

The system supports:

- chip inventory by denomination
- drop / fill tracking
- bank summary
- rundown report

This section should make clear that money movement is tied back to the operating table context and end-of-shift review.

### Page purpose of this pillar

To show that individual events accumulate into a coherent table-level record.

---

## 7.5 Keep Attribution Attached

Money entries and confirmations need authorship.

The page should show that cash accountability includes:

- staff attribution on cash actions
- reversal context
- confirmation context
- enough structure to see who recorded, who confirmed, and what changed

This section should stay operational.

Do not let it turn into a broad “audit trail philosophy” sermon.

### Page purpose of this pillar

To show that cash activity can be reviewed later because authorship and event context were preserved at the time of entry.

---

**Cash movement recorded in real time**

Immediate, operational, current-tense.

---

### Tone

### Content focus
- fills
- credits
- confirmations

---

- bank summary
- rundown report

## 9. Tone Guidance

The tone of this page should be:

- operational
- current-tense
- controlled
- specific
- accountability-focused

It should avoid:

- turning into a compliance lecture
- feature cramming
- vague “financial intelligence” filler
- generic ERP/accounting software language
- sermonizing about auditability in the abstract

The page should sound like it was written by someone who understands that money movement gets messy on a live floor and must be captured before memory and handoffs distort it.

## 11. Exclusions for Drafting

Do not let the page get hijacked by these topics:

- deep MTL / CTR explanation
- shift dashboard and floor-wide KPI storytelling
- Player 360 detail
- setup wizard and import detail
- loyalty issuance / redemption
- full role-permission matrix
- full table custody lifecycle

Those belong to other supporting pages.

---

## 12. Success Criteria

The Cash Accountability page is successful if:

1. it is understandable in under 15 seconds
2. it feels like a live operating loop, not a finance software module
3. it clearly centers real-time money movement and confirmation
4. it ties individual cash events back to table context
5. it shows enough attribution and exception handling to feel trustworthy without becoming a compliance essay

---

## 13. Final Summary

**Cash Accountability** should establish one clean idea:

> The system records cash movement while it happens, preserves reversals and confirmations as part of the same history, and ties money activity back to table context so the shift can be reviewed without guesswork.

That is enough.

Do not let the page swell into a second compliance page.

## 1. Purpose

This artifact replaces the earlier prose-only inset.

The earlier inset correctly identified useful copy techniques:

- action-first language,
- operational consequence before mechanism,
- proof-oriented prose,
- avoidance of generic SaaS language.

However, the Linear homepage / Monitor review revealed that the stronger governing principle is not merely prose style.

The stronger principle is:

> narrative containment.

The landing page must be governed as a bounded operational narrative, not as a copywriting exercise.

## 1.1 Reconciliation With FIB-H-LP-001

This directive does not expand the scope of `FIB-H-LP-001`.

It refines how the FIB should be executed.

The FIB remains authoritative for:

- the operator problem,
- the primary buyer,
- the operational-intelligence positioning,
- the exclusion of AI-first, pricing, onboarding, and full redesign work,
- the requirement that floor activity becomes oversight, accountability, and insight.

This directive is authoritative for:

- narrative pacing,
- cognitive containment,
- section-to-section movement,
- buyer-facing terminology,
- main-page prose density,
- screenshot burden-sharing,
- main-page vs. derived-surface boundary.

Where earlier FIB language refers to separate accountability, provenance, and product-surface stages, this directive reconciles the direction as:

```text
Operations
→ product proof
→ operational accountability
→ operational intelligence
```

That reconciliation preserves the FIB outcome while reducing main-page cognitive load.

## 1.2 Continuous Narrative Movement Rule

The landing page should feel like one continuous operational walkthrough.

A new section must feel like:
- a deeper layer of the same operational reality,

not:
- a new product pitch,
- a new ontology branch,
- or a restart of platform explanation.

The reader should feel continuous forward movement,
not repeated conceptual resets.

## 2. Discovery Summary

Linear's homepage and Monitor page demonstrate a disciplined narrative pattern:

```text
Operational outcome
→ bounded workflow concern
→ compact contextual explanation
→ visual proof
→ next scoped layer
```

The pattern is not classic SaaS feature marketing.

It is:

```text
category framing
→ operational worldview
→ workflow continuity
→ compressed proof
→ progressive zoom
→ bounded expansion
```

It does not attempt to explain the entire product at once.

It does not restart the narrative in each section.

It does not over-explain architecture.

It uses short copy blocks and lets screenshots carry part of the explanatory burden.

The result is a page that feels coherent because every section remains inside one cognitive frame.

The strongest finding is that the page behaves like one continuous camera movement.

Each section makes the same system come into clearer focus.

It does not feel like:

```text
new section = new product pitch
```

It feels like:

```text
same operational reality → deeper focus
```

That is the target for the landing-page refactor.

The refactored page must become:

```text
one operational system revealed progressively
```

not:

```text
module → module → module → module
```

The landing page should adopt this adapted pattern:

```text
↓
```

The page should progressively zoom into operational reality.

It must not repeatedly zoom back out into generalized product explanation.

## 3.1 Progressive Contextualization Rule

Each section should:

```text
introduce one capability or consequence
→ ground it in floor operations
→ validate it visually or concretely
→ move to the next adjacent layer
```

It must not:

- fully explain the entire mechanism,
- restart the product category,
- enumerate every related capability,
- promote adjacent subclaims into new peer concepts.

The page should let the buyer infer coherence from sequencing.

It should not repeatedly state why the architecture is coherent.

## 3.2 Semantic Adjacency Rule

Every main-page section must remain emotionally and cognitively adjacent to the prior section.

Approved sequence:

```text
operations
→ product surfaces
→ accountability
→ intelligence
```

Rejected sequence pattern:

```text
operations
→ compliance
→ dashboards
→ trust
→ analytics
→ provenance
```

The rejected pattern creates semantic branch explosion.

The approved pattern preserves forward movement.

## 4. Canonical Principle

The landing page should not explain the system ontology.

It should demonstrate the operational reality the system creates.

The buyer should leave understanding:

> floor activity becomes operational visibility, accountability, and managerial insight.

The buyer should not feel like they were taught:

> the platform's internal operational ontology.

This is not traditional storytelling.

The goal is not to entertain, dramatize, or emotionally manipulate.

The goal is structured operational cognition:

- reduce ambiguity,
- guide inference,
- preserve one frame,
- show enough proof for the buyer to keep moving.

---

## 5. Progressive Operational Zoom Rule

The page must move through one continuous zoom sequence:

### 5.1 Hero
Answer:

> What operational outcomes does this system create?

### 5.2 Floor Operations
Answer:

> What floor workflows does the system control or clarify?

### 5.3 Product Surfaces
Answer:

> What does this look like in the actual product?

### 5.4 Operational Accountability
Answer:

> Can operational leadership trust the record under review?

### 5.5 Operational Intelligence
Answer:

> What insight becomes possible once the floor record is continuous?

### 5.6 CTA
Answer:

> How does the buyer evaluate this against their own operation?

Each section deepens the same operational narrative.

No section may restart the product explanation.

### 5.7 Section transition requirement

Every substantive section should include or imply one forward transition.

The transition should answer:

> Why does the next layer naturally follow from this one?

Examples:

- Operations naturally lead to product proof because the buyer needs to see the workflow.
- Product proof naturally leads to accountability because visible workflows create reviewable records.
- Accountability naturally leads to intelligence because trusted records make shift insight credible.

Do not use explicit topology copy where sequencing can do the work.

---

## 6. One Cognitive Frame Per Section

Each section must answer one operational question only.

Reject any section that tries to combine:

- product category,
- feature inventory,
- workflow explanation,
- compliance argument,
- analytics argument,
- architecture explanation,

inside the same block.

If a section needs more than one cognitive frame, split it or move the extra material to a derived surface.

### Practical test

A section is contained if it can be summarized as:

```text
This section shows how [one operational concern] is handled.
```

A section is drifting if it requires:

```text
This section shows how [concern A], [concern B], and [architecture concept C] interact.
```

That is how the page turns back into a damned topology lecture.

---

## 7. Main-Page Prose Density Constraint

Main-page prose must remain visually scannable.

### Rules

- No supporting paragraph should exceed approximately 2–3 rendered desktop lines.
- Content blocks should communicate one operational idea only.
- Avoid stacked abstractions inside one sentence.
- Prefer one strong operational claim over three adjacent claims.
- Long explanatory prose belongs on derived surfaces, not the main page.
- If a section needs multiple paragraphs to justify itself, the section boundary is probably wrong.

### Copy smell

Bad:

```text
The platform establishes a unified operational accountability layer by connecting telemetry, provenance, compliance visibility, and intelligence surfaces into one downstream system of record.
```

Better:

```text
Floor activity stays tied to people, tables, and the gaming day.
```

---

## 8. Screenshot Burden-Sharing Rule

Screenshots are part of the argument structure.

They are not decorative.

A screenshot should absorb explanation burden by showing:

- workflow continuity,
- operational visibility,
- staff/table/session context,
- cash/accountability context,
- exception visibility,
- shift-level operational state.

If prose must explain what the screenshot should have made obvious, the screenshot is either wrong, poorly sequenced, or insufficiently annotated.

### Screenshot rule

Each main product-surface block should follow:

```text
Short operational claim
→ screenshot
→ one-line operational interpretation
```

Not:

```text
Long explanation
→ generic screenshot
→ more explanation
```

## 9. Narrative Containment Rules

### 9.1 Do not explain architecture

Avoid visible phrases such as:

- operational ontology,
- telemetry pipeline,
- projection layer,
- derived state,
- propagation,
- event topology,
- financial provenance,
- data architecture.

These may remain internal scaffold language.

They should not become buyer-facing copy.

### 9.2 Do not reopen the whole product

Each section should continue the story.

It should not say, in effect:

> Here is another way to think about the whole platform.

That creates narrative fatigue.

### 9.3 Do not promote subclaims into peer concepts

Compliance, cash accountability, audit trail, and operational trust are related buyer concerns.

They should not be fragmented into too many peer sections.

For the main page, they belong under:

```text
Operational Accountability
```

### 9.4 Do not overclaim financial authority

The page may communicate:

- cash accountability,
- operational financial visibility,
- attributed financial activity,
- audit trail,
- threshold visibility.

The page must not imply:

- settlement truth,
- accounting finality,
- reconciliation authority,
- final drop calculation,
- full custody reconstruction.

Translate internal ontology into floor language.

### Preferred terms

- table activity
- shift visibility
- session continuity
- cash accountability
- operational audit trail
- staff attribution
- gaming day
- supervisor review
- threshold visibility
- operational history
- floor-control workflow

### Avoid buyer-facing use

- provenance, unless carefully qualified
- telemetry, unless paired with operational explanation
- ontology
- event propagation
- projection
- infrastructure
- mutation
- derived state
- canonical layer

## 11. Action-First Copy Rule

Use operational verbs.

### Preferred verbs

- Track
- Surface
- Preserve
- Attribute
- Review
- Detect
- Monitor
- Verify
- Record
- Trace
- Reconstruct
- Escalate
- Resolve
- Follow

### Avoid generic SaaS verbs

- Leverage
- Utilize
- Empower
- Reimagine
- Revolutionize
- Optimize synergies
- Unlock
- Supercharge
- AI-powered

The page should sound like operational software for serious casino leadership, not like a venture-backed toaster wrote it.

## 11.1 Consequence-First Heading Rule

Rendered page headings should prefer operational consequences over taxonomy labels.

Scaffold labels may remain internal section names.

Buyer-facing headings should avoid sounding like:

```text
Operational Accountability
Operational Intelligence
Financial Provenance
Workflow Infrastructure
```

Prefer consequence phrasing:

```text
Activity tied to people, tables, and the gaming day.
See what changed across the shift.
Cash movement visible while the floor is still active.
Review the record without reconstructing the shift.
```

The test:

> Would an operator immediately understand what changed on the floor?

If not, the heading is probably too ontology-forward.

---

## 12. Bullet and Block-Length Rule

Linear's pattern demonstrates aggressive visual compression.

Adopt the same discipline.

### Main-page bullet rules

- Bullets should generally stay under 3 rendered lines.
- Prefer 1–2 lines when possible.
- A bullet should contain one operational point.
- Do not chain multiple clauses with semicolons.
- If a bullet exceeds 3 lines, it likely belongs on a derived surface.

### Main-page content-block rule

Each block should contain:

```text
Heading: 2–6 words
Copy: 1 compact sentence or 2 short sentences
```

Avoid content blocks that become mini-essays.

### 12.1 Scanning Velocity Rule

The main page must optimize for scanning velocity before full comprehension.

Assumption:

```text
buyers scan first
infer second
commit attention third
```

Therefore:

- screenshots should carry more meaning than prose,
- whitespace should separate cognitive moves,
- section copy should create momentum,
- derived surfaces should absorb detail,
- no section should require slow reading to understand why it exists.

If the buyer must stop and parse the architecture, the section is failing.

## 13. Section-Level Pattern

Each substantive section should use this pattern:

```text
Section heading:
  operational outcome

Subheading:
  one sentence connecting the outcome to floor activity

Blocks:
  3–4 contained proof points max

Visual:
  screenshot, workflow card, or operational UI evidence

Transition:
  one line that moves to the next zoom layer
```

This preserves movement.

It prevents the page from becoming a static capability catalog.

### 13.1 Camera-Movement Test

Review the page as if it were one continuous camera movement across the operation.

Each section should feel like:

```text
the camera moves closer to the same operating reality
```

not:

```text
the camera cuts to a different product pitch
```

If a transition feels like a hard cut, either:

- the section is in the wrong order,
- the heading is too categorical,
- the proof is too abstract,
- or the content belongs on a derived surface.

---

## 14. Main Page vs Derived Surface Boundary

The main page is the spine.

Derived surfaces carry depth.

### Main page may include

- core positioning,
- compressed operational claims,
- screenshot proof,
- one CTA path,
- high-level accountability/intelligence framing.

### Derived surfaces should carry

- workflow detail,
- deeper screenshots,
- implementation-adjacent explanations,
- domain-specific examples,
- compliance/cash/session subflows,
- long-form operational proof.

If a main-page section starts needing too much explanation, move that material into the relevant derived surface.

## 16. Remaining Copy Corrections to Apply

Replace:

```text
No anonymous mutations.
```

With:

```text
No anonymous edits.
```

or:


### 16.2 Avoid “estimated” as casual marketing contrast

Replace:

```text
shift intelligence is grounded — not estimated.
```

With:

```text
shift intelligence is tied to live operational activity.
```

or:

```text
shift intelligence reflects what is happening on the floor as it happens.
```

With:

```text
Operational accountability that holds up during review.
```

```text
Activity tied to people, tables, and the gaming day.
```

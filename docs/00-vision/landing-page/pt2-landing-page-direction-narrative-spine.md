# PT-2 Landing Page Direction — Narrative Spine, Not Feature Dump

**Date:** 2026-04-10  
**Purpose:** Capture the revised landing-page direction: keep the homepage contained, move explanatory density into supporting pages, and separate operating narrative from UI proof.

---

## 1. Decision

**Do not place the Operating Loops inside the Product section.**

That would collapse two distinct jobs into one surface:

- **Operating Loops** explain what the operator can do and how the system fits live floor work.
- **Product** proves that real UI surfaces exist to support those operating loops.

These are related, but they are not the same thing.

The homepage should not attempt to explain the entire application in one pass. It should establish a coherent narrative, then route the visitor into deeper pages where needed.

---

## 2. Strategic Shift

The current landing direction is trying to do too much on one page:

- position the category
- explain operational workflows
- inventory major features
- show screenshots
- build trust
- answer objections

That creates conceptual crowding.

The revised direction is:

**Main page = narrative spine**  
**Supporting pages = explanatory expansion**

This is closer to the Linear pattern: the homepage communicates a clear operational story, while deeper pages carry the denser explanation.

---

## 3. Structural Principle

The homepage should stay at the level of:

1. **What this system is**
2. **What operating loops it supports**
3. **What the product looks like**
4. **Why it can be trusted**
5. **What the next step is**

Everything else should be moved off-page where appropriate.

---

## 4. Section Architecture

## 4.1 Hero

Keep the hero stable for now.

Hero band:

- Floor Oversight
- Session Tracking
- Cash Accountability
- Audit Compliance

This band should act as a **top-level taxonomy**, not as four separate long homepage sections.

It establishes the operational world the product lives in.

---

## 4.2 Operating Loops

Place this **before Product**.

This section should not be a feature inventory.  
It should be a **narrative router**.

Its job is to answer:

> What can this system help me do on a live casino floor?

Use the four hero-band domains as the backbone:

- **Floor Oversight**
- **Session Tracking**
- **Cash Accountability**
- **Audit Compliance**

Each loop should contain:

- a short outcome statement
- a short operational-motion statement
- a link to a deeper page

### Example shape

**Floor Oversight**  
See the floor as it is now, not after the shift is over.  
Track tables, coverage, checkpoints, and exceptions as play moves.  
**Explore floor oversight →**

This keeps the main page restrained while still creating an intelligible path into the product.

---

## 4.3 Product

Keep Product as the **UI proof section**.

Product should not carry the conceptual burden of explaining the app.  
Its role is narrower:

- show credible UI surfaces
- support claims already introduced above
- make the system feel real

So the distinction becomes:

- **Operating Loops** = story of use
- **Product** = visual proof
- **Supporting pages** = deeper explanation

That separation reduces clutter and keeps Product from becoming a screenshot landfill.

---

## 4.4 Trust

Trust remains its own section.

This is where the page communicates the system’s structural properties, such as:

- every number is traceable
- every action is attributed
- nothing gets rewritten
- anomalies surface during operations

This section answers:

> Why should this system be trusted in live operations?

It should not drift into feature explanation.

---

## 4.5 CTA

The close should remain outcome-oriented and direct.

The CTA should follow a page sequence that feels like:

- here is the system
- here is how it fits operations
- here is what it looks like
- here is why it is credible
- here is what to do next

---

## 5. What Should Leave the Homepage

The following content should be candidates for **supporting pages**, not expanded inline on the homepage:

- detailed workflow explanation
- role-by-role breakdown
- setup/import specifics
- compliance mechanics
- cashier operational detail
- admin configuration detail
- deeper player/profile narratives
- full feature exhaust

This is the main anti-convolution move.

The homepage should suggest range, not dump inventory.

---

## 6. Recommended Site Shape

## Main landing page

1. Hero
2. Operating Loops
3. Product
4. Trust
5. CTA

## Supporting pages

- `/floor-oversight`
- `/session-tracking`
- `/cash-accountability`
- `/audit-compliance`

Optional later pages:

- `/setup-and-import`
- `/player-360`
- `/compliance-and-audit-trail`

---

## 7. Why This Direction Is Better

This structure fixes the current problem by assigning one job to each section:

- **Hero** sets category and frame
- **Operating Loops** explains what can be done
- **Product** shows real surfaces
- **Trust** establishes structural credibility
- **CTA** closes the page cleanly

That is materially cleaner than trying to explain the app, prove the app, and inventory the app on the same surface.

---

## 8. Working Rule Going Forward

When evaluating a homepage section, ask:

> Is this section telling the operating story, proving the product, or expanding detail?

If it is trying to do more than one of those at once, it is likely bloated and should be split or moved off-page.

---

## 9. Immediate Next Step

Do not redesign the whole landing page at once.

Instead:

- keep the hero stable
- define the **Operating Loops** section precisely
- select one supporting page to design first

Recommended first supporting page:

**Floor Oversight**

Reason:
- it is operationally central
- it is easy to understand
- it gives a strong template for the other loop pages

---

## 10. Final Summary

The homepage should be a **contained operational narrative**, not a compressed manual.

The right split is:

- **Operating Loops before Product**
- **Product as proof, not explanation**
- **Supporting pages for depth**

That is the direction to continue with.
